The Streisand Effect is a social phenomenon where any attempt to hide, remove, censor, or obfuscate information backfires, causing many more people to know about the information than if no hiding had took place.

Psychologists often attribute this to psychological reactance, where people become more interested in the information once they realize it's being withheld from them.

It's named after Barbra Streisand, who in 2003 sued photographer Kenneth Adelman to remove an aerial photo of her Malibu mansion from the California Coastal Records Project (a project seeking to document coastal erosion). Before her lawsuit, only a handful of people had actually seen the photo. However, once news of the viral legal action spread, hundreds of thousands of people visited the site within a month to view the photo.
